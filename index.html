    <section id="overview">

      <h2>Project Overview</h2>

      <p>

        Medication administration errors are a major patient safety concern and can increase

        morbidity, length of stay, and healthcare costs. This quality improvement (QI)

        initiative was designed to reduce medication administration errors on a 32-bed

        medical-surgical unit by applying a structured QI framework, improving processes, and

        engaging frontline staff.

      </p>

    </section>


    <section id="problem">

      <h2>Problem Statement & Background</h2>

      <p>

        Baseline data from January–March 2024 showed an average of

        <strong>8 reported medication administration errors per 1,000 medication doses</strong>

        on the unit. The most common issues involved wrong time, missed doses, and documentation

        errors. Incident reports and staff feedback highlighted system-level barriers such as

        inconsistent workflows and BCMA device issues.

      </p>

    </section>


    <section id="aim">

      <h2>Aim & Objectives</h2>

      <h3>SMART Aim</h3>

      <p>

        To reduce reported medication administration errors on the medical-surgical unit by

        <strong>50% (from 8 to 4 per 1,000 doses)</strong> by

        <strong>December 31, 2024</strong>, while maintaining or improving staff satisfaction

        and reporting culture.

      </p>


      <h3>Objectives</h3>

      <ul>

        <li>Standardize the medication administration process across all shifts.</li>

        <li>Improve adherence to barcode medication administration (BCMA) policies.</li>

        <li>Enhance staff knowledge and confidence related to safe medication administration.</li>

        <li>Promote a culture of safety and non-punitive error reporting.</li>

      </ul>

    </section>


    <section id="methods">

      <h2>Methods</h2>


      <h3>QI Framework</h3>

      <p>

        This project utilized the <strong>Plan-Do-Study-Act (PDSA)</strong> cycle to test

        and refine interventions in small, rapid cycles before wider implementation.

      </p>


      <h3>Measures</h3>

      <ul>

        <li><strong>Outcome:</strong> Medication administration errors per 1,000 doses.</li>

        <li><strong>Process:</strong> BCMA scan rate; on-time medication administration.</li>

        <li><strong>Balancing:</strong> Nurse-reported workload, satisfaction, and time per med pass.</li>

      </ul>


      <h3>Data Sources</h3>

      <ul>

        <li>Incident reporting system</li>

        <li>Electronic health record (EHR)</li>

        <li>Anonymous staff surveys</li>

      </ul>


      <h3>Key Interventions</h3>

      <ol>

        <li>

          <strong>Standardized Medication Administration Checklist</strong>  

          Co-developed with frontline nurses and incorporated into daily practice.

        </li>

        <li>

          <strong>BCMA Optimization</strong>  

          Ensured functional scanners, addressed technical issues, and provided quick-reference guides.

        </li>

        <li>

          <strong>Targeted Education Sessions</strong>  

          Brief, case-based in-services focusing on high-risk medications and common error modes.

        </li>

        <li>

          <strong>Visual Job Aids</strong>  

          Posters and badge cards outlining the “5+2 Rights” and unit-specific safety tips.

        </li>

        <li>

          <strong>Data Feedback & Recognition</strong>  

          Monthly safety huddles sharing updated metrics and recognizing improvement efforts.

        </li>

      </ol>

    </section>


    <section id="results">

      <h2>Results</h2>


      <div class="metrics">

        <div class="metric-card">

          <strong>Medication Errors</strong>

          Decreased from 8 to 3.5 per 1,000 doses over 9 months.

        </div>

        <div class="metric-card">

          <strong>BCMA Adherence</strong>

          Improved from 78% to 95% of doses scanned.

        </div>

        <div class="metric-card">

          <strong>On-time Administration</strong>

          Improved from 65% to 82% within the 60-minute window.

        </div>

      </div>


      <h3>Quantitative Findings</h3>

      <p>

        Run charts demonstrated a sustained downward shift in medication administration errors

        following implementation of the standardized checklist and BCMA optimization. Special

        cause variation was observed beginning in month 4, corresponding to combined

        interventions.

      </p>


      <h3>Qualitative Feedback</h3>

      <ul>

        <li>Nurses reported greater confidence in medication safety practices.</li>

        <li>Perceived communication with pharmacy improved.</li>

        <li>

          Increased near-miss reporting indicated a more open and proactive safety culture.

        </li>

      </ul>

    </section>


    <section id="lessons">

      <h2>Lessons Learned & Sustainability</h2>


      <h3>Lessons Learned</h3>

      <ul>

        <li>

          Early involvement of frontline staff in intervention design was critical for buy-in

          and adoption.

        </li>

        <li>

          Addressing technical and workflow barriers (e.g., scanner availability) enabled

          behavior change.

        </li>

        <li>

          Regular feedback loops and recognition helped maintain engagement and momentum.

        </li>

      </ul>


      <h3>Sustainability Plan</h3>

      <ul>

        <li>Embed the medication safety checklist into orientation for all new nurses.</li>

        <li>Continue monthly safety huddles and visual display of run charts on the unit.</li>

        <li>Spread successful interventions to additional units within the organization.</li>

      </ul>
